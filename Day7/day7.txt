The locations that are isolated from each other but close enough to have low-latency connections with each other are known as availability zones.
AZs represent parts of regions, and each AZ includes one or more data center.


In cloud computing, an availability zone (AZ) is a group of data centers within
 a region, which is a geographic area. AZs are isolated locations within a region,
 each region has at least three AZs. AZs are connected by low-latency links to 
 provide fault tolerance and replication.
